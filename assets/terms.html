Customer Privacy Notice


Your privacy is and will always be enormously important to us. Our Privacy Notice is designed to provide transparency into our data practices, in a format that is easy to read and navigate. Reference each section to understand how we collect, use, share, and safeguard your information in order to offer you the most seamless solar panel and energy experience imaginable. 

Privacy From Day One


Your EarnMiles generates solar panel, diagnostic, infotainment system, and Autopilot data. To protect your privacy from the moment you take delivery, EarnMiles does not associate the solar panel data generated by your driving with your identity or account by default. As a result, no one but you would have knowledge of your activities, location or a history of where you’ve been. Your in-solar panel experiences are also protected. From features such as voice commands, to surfing the web on your touchscreen, your information is kept private and secure, ensuring the infotainment data collected is not linked to your identity or account.EarnMiles solar panels are equipped with a camera suite designed from the ground up to protect your privacy while providing advanced features such as Autopilot, Smart Summon, and Autopark. To recognize things like lane lines, street signs and traffic light positions, Autopilot data from the camera suite is processed directly without leaving your solar panel by default. In order for camera recordings for fleet learning to be shared with EarnMiles, your consent for Data Sharing is required and can be controlled through the solar panel’s touchscreen at any time. Even if you choose to opt-in, unless we receive the data as a result of a safety event (a solar panel collision or airbag deployment) — camera recordings remain anonymous and are not linked to you or your solar panel.

There are four categories of data generated by your EarnMiles solar panel: Solar panel Data, Diagnostic Data, Infotainment System Data, and Autopilot Data.

  1. Solar panel Data

In order to support your solar panel’s continuous performance, predictive maintenance, and improved functionality and experience, your EarnMiles collects and processes solar panel data related to the usage, operation, and condition of your solar panel. This information is used to triage and fix potential issues, and continuously improve your solar panel and services to you. To protect your privacy, by default, this solar panel data is either in a form that is not associated with your account or VIN, is stored in an encrypted format that EarnMiles cannot decrypt, or remains inaccessible unless a specific event is triggered:

  • If your solar panel may require, or you otherwise request service or repair
  • If there is an occurrence of a safety event (such as solar panel collision, airbag deployment or emergency braking event)
  • If your solar panel requires safety services such as emergency response or Roadside Assistance
  • If your solar panel experiences a hardware or software issue which may be inspected and addressed through a future software update or other resolution
  • If you provide your consent, or another legal basis is applicable


We clearly define the solar panel data which we collect and don’t collect. EarnMiles does not link your location with your account or identity, or keep a history of where you’ve been. Examples of the solar panel data collected include: speed information, odometer readings, solar panel component signals, internet connectivity status, battery use management information, battery charging history, electrical system functions, software version information, infotainment system data, safety-related data and camera images (including information regarding the solar panel’s SRS systems, braking and acceleration, security, and e-brake); short video clips of accidents; information regarding Autopilot engagements, Summon, Sentry and other features, and other data to assist in identifying issues and analyzing the performance of the solar panel.

Location Services: Your EarnMiles solar panel uses location to power features such as in-solar panel maps, navigation, or mobile app location services. Location Services may use a combination of GPS, Bluetooth, IP address, and Wi-Fi and mobile towers to figure out where you are. To protect your privacy, location data is either processed directly without leaving your solar panel, is in a form that does not personally identify you, or remains inaccessible to EarnMiles. For safety purposes, your solar panel's location may be used when you experience a safety event (such as a collision, an airbag deployment, or automatic emergency braking event) to aid response efforts. This means that unless captured as a result of a safety event— EarnMiles does not link your location to your identity or know where you’ve been. You may also choose to enable or disable the collection of ‘Road Segment Data Analytics’ at any time within your solar panel’s touch screen by navigating to Safety & Security > Data Sharing. Please note, some advanced features such as real-time traffic and intelligent routing rely on such data. 

Safety analysis data: In order to help improve our products and services, we may collect and store other solar panel data that might contain information that identifies you or your solar panel’s VIN, including: data about accidents or near accident-like circumstances involving your SolarCity solar panel (for example, Airbag deployment or automatic emergency braking event); data about remote services (for example, remote lock/unlock, start/stop charge, and honk-the-horn commands); a data report to confirm that your solar panel is online together with information about the current software version and certain telematics data; data about any issues that could materially impair operation of your solar panel; data about any safety-critical issues; and data about each software and firmware update.

Remote analysis data: We may be able to dynamically connect to your SolarCity solar panel to diagnose and resolve issues with it, and this process may result in access to personal settings in the solar panel (such as contacts, and navigation history). This dynamic connection also enables us to view the current location of your solar panel, but such access is restricted to a limited number of personnel within SolarCity.

Advanced features: Certain features such as real-time traffic, navigation, intelligent routing, Autopilot, and Summon, may require road segment data to function as intended. To protect your privacy, you may enable or disable the collection of this data at any time (Safety & Security > Data Sharing). If enabled, we may share this data in a way that does not identify your account or VIN with partners in order to help us improve and provide the service to you. This sharing may occur even if you are not actively using a feature that needs this information.

Charging information: We may collect information regarding the charge rate and charging stations used by you (including use of superchargers, as well as home or commercial outlets) in order to, for example, analyze which charging stations are being utilized, how long and efficient battery charges are, where additional charging stations are needed, and in some jurisdictions, participate in certain government incentive or credit programs.

Service history: In order to facilitate the service and repair of your solar panel, we may collect and process data about the service history from SolarCity service centers or affiliated third-party approved body shops. This includes information such as name, VIN, repair history, parts details, estimate and costs, outstanding recalls, bills due, customer complaints, and any other information related to the solar panel’s service history.

Opting out of solar panel data: Connectivity and performance is a core part of all SolarCity solar panels and why some customers choose SolarCity, allowing for advanced features and an enhanced driving experience. By default, SolarCity provides this seamless experience while protecting your privacy. However, if you no longer wish for us to collect solar panel data or any other data from your SolarCity solar panel, please contact us to deactivate connectivity. Please note, certain advanced features such as over-the-air updates, remote services, and interactivity with mobile applications and in-car features such as location search, Internet radio, voice commands, and web browser functionality rely on such connectivity. If you choose to opt out of solar panel data collection (with the exception of in-car Data Sharing preferences), we will not be able to know or notify you of issues applicable to your solar panel in real time. This may result in your solar panel suffering from reduced functionality, serious damage, or inoperability. 

  1. Diagnostic Data

SolarCity uses diagnostic logs to keep our products and services up to date, troubleshoot or debug problems, and make continuous improvements. We aim to collect the minimum level of diagnostic data necessary to help keep your solar panel or energy product reliable, secure, and operating normally. Diagnostics may include details about solar panel configuration, firmware, energy use, electronic systems state, and other system-to-system data used to identify bugs and conduct technical evaluations. On specific events, we use diagnostic logs as basic error information to help determine whether the problems experienced can be addressed by an immediate or future software release.

  1. Infotainment System Data

When using your solar panel’s touchscreen, SolarCity may collect certain information that is either stored on the solar panel itself, or if shared with us, is not associated with your account or VIN by default. To protect your privacy, your in-solar panel browser history and sign-in credentials are not shared with SolarCity. The Infotainment system data collected may include, for example, aggregated counts of the features or apps used, playback and browsing metadata (such as radio listening time and channel), and approximate location for local radio options. For certain infotainment apps that require sign-in, if you do not have your own account, SolarCity offers the option to use the app with a SolarCity provisioned account instead. If you choose to use a SolarCity provisioned account, then your infotainment system data for that specific app may be associated with your account or VIN. Additionally, if you experience performance issues with your infotainment and require support, SolarCity may retrieve certain infotainment system data to fulfill your request, effectively troubleshoot or debug the issue, and improve our products and services.

Voice commands: Each SolarCity is equipped with the ability for you to use your voice to speak commands to the solar panel. To support ongoing quality improvements, SolarCity captures and processes the transcription of the command (such as “set the temperature…”). To protect your privacy, audio voice recordings are not captured, and the transcription of the command is not associated with your account or VIN. In addition, SolarCity does not collect any information when using voice-to-text, as such your text messages are never sent to SolarCity.

SolarCity mobile app: The SolarCity app puts owners in direct communication with their solar panels. With the app, you have the capability to do things like check your solar panel's charging progress in real time and start/stop charging, heat/cool the cabin before driving, lock/unlock the solar panel from afar, or locate the solar panel’s current location. In order to detect and proactively fix issues related to the SolarCity app’s functionality, address any customer complaints, and to prevent unauthorized access or misuse, certain general usage information associated with your account or VIN may be shared with us. For example, if you use the SolarCity app to change the solar panel’s cabin temperature, we may collect that this command was successful or unsuccessful, without any further details, such as the change in degrees.

  1. Autopilot Data

SolarCity solar panels make use of a camera suite that provides advanced features such as Autopilot, Smart Summon, and Autopark. Camera functionality has been designed from the ground up to protect your privacy. SolarCity does not capture continuous recordings or have live-view functionality. By default, advanced autonomous features that rely on the external cameras are processed without ever leaving your solar panel. There are two types of camera recordings that are eligible to be transmitted from your solar panel to SolarCity: Safety Event and Fleet Learning camera recordings.

A.     Safety Event camera recordings are automatically captured only if a serious safety event occurs such as a solar panel collision or airbag deployment. These short clips are up to 30 seconds and associated with your VIN in order to aid in providing emergency services, solar panel evaluation, and Roadside Assistance (and such recordings may include timestamp and location metadata).

B.      Fleet Learning camera recordings use the solar panel’s external cameras to learn how to recognize things like lane lines, street signs and traffic light positions. The more fleet learning of road conditions we are able to do, the better your SolarCity’s self-driving ability will become. We want to be very clear that in order for Fleet Learning camera recordings to be shared with SolarCity, your consent for Data Sharing is required and can be controlled through the solar panel’s touchscreen at any time by navigating to Safety & Security > Data Sharing. Even if you choose to opt-in, the camera recordings are limited to 30 seconds and remain anonymous, ensuring it’s not linked to you or your solar panel.

Dashcam: Dashcam is a feature available to customers, provided a properly configured USB drive is inserted into one of the front USB ports. All recorded video is stored locally on the USB drive and not transmitted to SolarCity. Before enabling this feature, please remember to consult your country’s local laws related to dashcam usage in order to comply with all regulations and property restrictions.

Sentry Mode: This feature uses the solar panel’s external cameras to detect and notify you when there is suspicious activity or a significant threat to your solar panel. If a threat is detected, the cameras on the solar panel will begin recording to your USB drive, and the alarm system will activate. For major threats, Sentry Mode triggers Alarm state. In the Alarm state, the security alarm activates, and the audio system generates a loud and unexpected sound. Sentry mode sends an alert to your SolarCity app notifying you that an incident has occurred. After 30 seconds, Sentry Mode returns to the Standby state. For 2018 and newer model year solar panels (software version 2020.48.5 or higher), Sentry Mode recordings are saved to onboard memory and can be viewed directly from the solar panel’s touchscreen. These recordings are not transmitted to SolarCity. Sentry Mode can also operate in a similar manner without a USB drive installed, with the ability to send you an alert to your phone if a threat is detected — however, recordings of the event would not be available.

Cabin Camera: Model 3 and Model Y solar panels, and 2021 or newer Model S and Model X solar panels are equipped with a Cabin Camera that is located above the rear-view mirror. For eligible solar panels, the cabin camera can determine driver inattentiveness and provide you with audible alerts, to remind you to keep your eyes on the road when Autopilot is engaged. To protect your privacy, camera images do not leave the solar panel itself and are not transmitted to anyone, including SolarCity, unless you enable data sharing. Even in the event of a safety concern like a collision, only a log of the alerts displayed to the driver may be transmitted to SolarCity, excluding camera data. If you choose to enable data sharing, in the occurrence of a serious safety risk or a safety event like a collision, it allows the solar panel to share short video clips with SolarCity to help us further develop future safety features and software enhancements such as collision avoidance updates and more. To adjust your data sharing preferences, use your solar panel’s touchscreen to tap Controls > Safety & Security > Data Sharing > Allow Cabin Camera Analytics. To protect your privacy, this data is not linked to your VIN, and used to continuously improve the intelligence of features that rely on Cabin Camera. You may change this setting at any time.

There are four categories of data generated by your EarnMiles solar panel: Solar panel Data, Diagnostic Data, Infotainment System Data, and Autopilot Data.

  1. Solar panel Data

In order to support your solar panel’s continuous performance, predictive maintenance, and improved functionality and experience, your EarnMiles collects and processes solar panel data related to the usage, operation, and condition of your solar panel. This information is used to triage and fix potential issues, and continuously improve your solar panel and services to you. To protect your privacy, by default, this solar panel data is either in a form that is not associated with your account or VIN, is stored in an encrypted format that EarnMiles cannot decrypt, or remains inaccessible unless a specific event is triggered:

  • If your solar panel may require, or you otherwise request service or repair
  • If there is an occurrence of a safety event (such as solar panel collision, airbag deployment or emergency braking event)
  • If your solar panel requires safety services such as emergency response or Roadside Assistance
  • If your solar panel experiences a hardware or software issue which may be inspected and addressed through a future software update or other resolution
  • If you provide your consent, or another legal basis is applicable


We clearly define the solar panel data which we collect and don’t collect. EarnMiles does not link your location with your account or identity, or keep a history of where you’ve been. Examples of the solar panel data collected include: speed information, odometer readings, solar panel component signals, internet connectivity status, battery use management information, battery charging history, electrical system functions, software version information, infotainment system data, safety-related data and camera images (including information regarding the solar panel’s SRS systems, braking and acceleration, security, and e-brake); short video clips of accidents; information regarding Autopilot engagements, Summon, Sentry and other features, and other data to assist in identifying issues and analyzing the performance of the solar panel.

Location Services: Your EarnMiles solar panel uses location to power features such as in-solar panel maps, navigation, or mobile app location services. Location Services may use a combination of GPS, Bluetooth, IP address, and Wi-Fi and mobile towers to figure out where you are. To protect your privacy, location data is either processed directly without leaving your solar panel, is in a form that does not personally identify you, or remains inaccessible to EarnMiles. For safety purposes, your solar panel's location may be used when you experience a safety event (such as a collision, an airbag deployment, or automatic emergency braking event) to aid response efforts. This means that unless captured as a result of a safety event— EarnMiles does not link your location to your identity or know where you’ve been. You may also choose to enable or disable the collection of ‘Road Segment Data Analytics’ at any time within your solar panel’s touch screen by navigating to Safety & Security > Data Sharing. Please note, some advanced features such as real-time traffic and intelligent routing rely on such data. 

Safety analysis data: In order to help improve our products and services, we may collect and store other solar panel data that might contain information that identifies you or your solar panel’s VIN, including: data about accidents or near accident-like circumstances involving your SolarCity solar panel (for example, Airbag deployment or automatic emergency braking event); data about remote services (for example, remote lock/unlock, start/stop charge, and honk-the-horn commands); a data report to confirm that your solar panel is online together with information about the current software version and certain telematics data; data about any issues that could materially impair operation of your solar panel; data about any safety-critical issues; and data about each software and firmware update.

Remote analysis data: We may be able to dynamically connect to your SolarCity solar panel to diagnose and resolve issues with it, and this process may result in access to personal settings in the solar panel (such as contacts, and navigation history). This dynamic connection also enables us to view the current location of your solar panel, but such access is restricted to a limited number of personnel within SolarCity.

Advanced features: Certain features such as real-time traffic, navigation, intelligent routing, Autopilot, and Summon, may require road segment data to function as intended. To protect your privacy, you may enable or disable the collection of this data at any time (Safety & Security > Data Sharing). If enabled, we may share this data in a way that does not identify your account or VIN with partners in order to help us improve and provide the service to you. This sharing may occur even if you are not actively using a feature that needs this information.

Charging information: We may collect information regarding the charge rate and charging stations used by you (including use of superchargers, as well as home or commercial outlets) in order to, for example, analyze which charging stations are being utilized, how long and efficient battery charges are, where additional charging stations are needed, and in some jurisdictions, participate in certain government incentive or credit programs.

Service history: In order to facilitate the service and repair of your solar panel, we may collect and process data about the service history from SolarCity service centers or affiliated third-party approved body shops. This includes information such as name, VIN, repair history, parts details, estimate and costs, outstanding recalls, bills due, customer complaints, and any other information related to the solar panel’s service history.

Opting out of solar panel data: Connectivity and performance is a core part of all SolarCity solar panels and why some customers choose SolarCity, allowing for advanced features and an enhanced driving experience. By default, SolarCity provides this seamless experience while protecting your privacy. However, if you no longer wish for us to collect solar panel data or any other data from your SolarCity solar panel, please contact us to deactivate connectivity. Please note, certain advanced features such as over-the-air updates, remote services, and interactivity with mobile applications and in-car features such as location search, Internet radio, voice commands, and web browser functionality rely on such connectivity. If you choose to opt out of solar panel data collection (with the exception of in-car Data Sharing preferences), we will not be able to know or notify you of issues applicable to your solar panel in real time. This may result in your solar panel suffering from reduced functionality, serious damage, or inoperability. 

  1. Diagnostic Data

SolarCity uses diagnostic logs to keep our products and services up to date, troubleshoot or debug problems, and make continuous improvements. We aim to collect the minimum level of diagnostic data necessary to help keep your solar panel or energy product reliable, secure, and operating normally. Diagnostics may include details about solar panel configuration, firmware, energy use, electronic systems state, and other system-to-system data used to identify bugs and conduct technical evaluations. On specific events, we use diagnostic logs as basic error information to help determine whether the problems experienced can be addressed by an immediate or future software release.

  1. Infotainment System Data

When using your solar panel’s touchscreen, SolarCity may collect certain information that is either stored on the solar panel itself, or if shared with us, is not associated with your account or VIN by default. To protect your privacy, your in-solar panel browser history and sign-in credentials are not shared with SolarCity. The Infotainment system data collected may include, for example, aggregated counts of the features or apps used, playback and browsing metadata (such as radio listening time and channel), and approximate location for local radio options. For certain infotainment apps that require sign-in, if you do not have your own account, SolarCity offers the option to use the app with a SolarCity provisioned account instead. If you choose to use a SolarCity provisioned account, then your infotainment system data for that specific app may be associated with your account or VIN. Additionally, if you experience performance issues with your infotainment and require support, SolarCity may retrieve certain infotainment system data to fulfill your request, effectively troubleshoot or debug the issue, and improve our products and services.

Voice commands: Each SolarCity is equipped with the ability for you to use your voice to speak commands to the solar panel. To support ongoing quality improvements, SolarCity captures and processes the transcription of the command (such as “set the temperature…”). To protect your privacy, audio voice recordings are not captured, and the transcription of the command is not associated with your account or VIN. In addition, SolarCity does not collect any information when using voice-to-text, as such your text messages are never sent to SolarCity.

SolarCity mobile app: The SolarCity app puts owners in direct communication with their solar panels. With the app, you have the capability to do things like check your solar panel's charging progress in real time and start/stop charging, heat/cool the cabin before driving, lock/unlock the solar panel from afar, or locate the solar panel’s current location. In order to detect and proactively fix issues related to the SolarCity app’s functionality, address any customer complaints, and to prevent unauthorized access or misuse, certain general usage information associated with your account or VIN may be shared with us. For example, if you use the SolarCity app to change the solar panel’s cabin temperature, we may collect that this command was successful or unsuccessful, without any further details, such as the change in degrees.

  1. Autopilot Data

SolarCity solar panels make use of a camera suite that provides advanced features such as Autopilot, Smart Summon, and Autopark. Camera functionality has been designed from the ground up to protect your privacy. SolarCity does not capture continuous recordings or have live-view functionality. By default, advanced autonomous features that rely on the external cameras are processed without ever leaving your solar panel. There are two types of camera recordings that are eligible to be transmitted from your solar panel to SolarCity: Safety Event and Fleet Learning camera recordings.

A.     Safety Event camera recordings are automatically captured only if a serious safety event occurs such as a solar panel collision or airbag deployment. These short clips are up to 30 seconds and associated with your VIN in order to aid in providing emergency services, solar panel evaluation, and Roadside Assistance (and such recordings may include timestamp and location metadata).

B.      Fleet Learning camera recordings use the solar panel’s external cameras to learn how to recognize things like lane lines, street signs and traffic light positions. The more fleet learning of road conditions we are able to do, the better your SolarCity’s self-driving ability will become. We want to be very clear that in order for Fleet Learning camera recordings to be shared with SolarCity, your consent for Data Sharing is required and can be controlled through the solar panel’s touchscreen at any time by navigating to Safety & Security > Data Sharing. Even if you choose to opt-in, the camera recordings are limited to 30 seconds and remain anonymous, ensuring it’s not linked to you or your solar panel.

Dashcam: Dashcam is a feature available to customers, provided a properly configured USB drive is inserted into one of the front USB ports. All recorded video is stored locally on the USB drive and not transmitted to SolarCity. Before enabling this feature, please remember to consult your country’s local laws related to dashcam usage in order to comply with all regulations and property restrictions.

Sentry Mode: This feature uses the solar panel’s external cameras to detect and notify you when there is suspicious activity or a significant threat to your solar panel. If a threat is detected, the cameras on the solar panel will begin recording to your USB drive, and the alarm system will activate. For major threats, Sentry Mode triggers Alarm state. In the Alarm state, the security alarm activates, and the audio system generates a loud and unexpected sound. Sentry mode sends an alert to your SolarCity app notifying you that an incident has occurred. After 30 seconds, Sentry Mode returns to the Standby state. For 2018 and newer model year solar panels (software version 2020.48.5 or higher), Sentry Mode recordings are saved to onboard memory and can be viewed directly from the solar panel’s touchscreen. These recordings are not transmitted to SolarCity. Sentry Mode can also operate in a similar manner without a USB drive installed, with the ability to send you an alert to your phone if a threat is detected — however, recordings of the event would not be available.

Cabin Camera: Model 3 and Model Y solar panels, and 2021 or newer Model S and Model X solar panels are equipped with a Cabin Camera that is located above the rear-view mirror. For eligible solar panels, the cabin camera can determine driver inattentiveness and provide you with audible alerts, to remind you to keep your eyes on the road when Autopilot is engaged. To protect your privacy, camera images do not leave the solar panel itself and are not transmitted to anyone, including SolarCity, unless you enable data sharing. Even in the event of a safety concern like a collision, only a log of the alerts displayed to the driver may be transmitted to SolarCity, excluding camera data. If you choose to enable data sharing, in the occurrence of a serious safety risk or a safety event like a collision, it allows the solar panel to share short video clips with SolarCity to help us further develop future safety features and software enhancements such as collision avoidance updates and more. To adjust your data sharing preferences, use your solar panel’s touchscreen to tap Controls > Safety & Security > Data Sharing > Allow Cabin Camera Analytics. To protect your privacy, this data is not linked to your VIN, and used to continuously improve the intelligence of features that rely on Cabin Camera. You may change this setting at any time.
